1.melatonin(褪黑素) 44295
This lecture focuses on melatonin(褪黑素),
a hormone(激素) made(合成) in the pineal gland(松果体),
a pea-sized(豌豆大小的) organ(器官) in the brain once called the "seat of soul(灵魂的宝座)" by Descartes. 
Melatonin(褪黑素) follows(follows) a rhythm(节奏) opposite to cortisol(皮质醇), 
peaking at night,(在晚上到达高峰) 
so it is known as the darkness hormone. 
It appears at night in all studied species(物种) and is linked to darkness-related behaviors such as human sleep and rodent(啮齿动物) activity(活动).

4.Bees' communication system
Bees use a waggle(摇摆) dance as their intricate communication system. 
When they find a good food source, they perform this dance to inform other bees in the hive about the location. 
A behavior study on their dancing behaviors shows that bees can use their dance and sounds to describe different flowers and the surrounding environment.

5.The Origin of Species
In the Origin of Species, 
Darwin proposed that life on Earth has evolved over time, 达尔文提出地球上的生命是随着时间进化的，
and natural selection is the primary mechanism(机制). 
But Darwin did not know precisely(精确地) how organisms pass heritable traits to their offspring. 但达尔文并不确切地知道生物体是如何将可遗传的特征传给后代的。
And Mendel proposed a model of inheritance in which organisms transmit discrete heritable units to their offspring. 
Mendel's paper set the stage for understanding the genetic differences on which evolution is based.
